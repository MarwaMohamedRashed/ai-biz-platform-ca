## What is LeapOne AEO?

**LeapOne AEO** answers one question for a small business owner:

> "When somebody asks ChatGPT, Perplexity, or Google for the best
> {your-business-type} in {your-city}, are you the answer?"

It scores your business out of 100 across the five things AI search engines actually look at, tells you exactly what is hurting your score, and gives you a prioritized to-do list to fix it. It also generates the on-page content (description, FAQs, schema markup) you need to start showing up.

Today most local businesses are invisible to AI search. The ones that *are* visible don't know why. LeapOne AEO turns "AI visibility" from a buzzword into a measurable score with a fix list.

## The AEO Readiness Score

A single number from **0 to 100**. Higher means more visible to AI search engines and AI-powered local search (ChatGPT, Perplexity, Google AI Overview, Google Maps).

| Score | Color | Meaning |
|---|---|---|
| 70–100 | Green | Strong AI visibility — you're being recommended |
| 40–69  | Amber | Real gaps — fixable in a weekend |
| 0–39   | Red   | AI engines essentially can't see you yet |

The score is broken down into **5 pillars**, each with a maximum number of points. The owner sees both the total and the per-pillar breakdown so they know where they stand.

| # | Pillar | Max | What it measures |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | Google Business Profile | 25 | Is your GBP claimed and complete (rating, category, contact)? |
| 2 | Reviews & Reputation | 22 | How many reviews, what star rating, are they recent? |
| 3 | Website & Schema | 20 | Is your site reachable, and does it have the structured data AI engines need? |
| 4 | Local Search Presence | 15 | Do you appear in Google's "Maps 3-pack" and regular search results? |
| 5 | AI Citation | 18 | Are you actually being cited by Perplexity and Google AI Overview? |

## The Recommendations Engine

A score by itself is not useful. The real product is the **prioritized fix list** the system generates after every audit. Each recommendation includes:

- **What pillar** it improves
- **A short headline** ("Get to 10+ Google reviews")
- **Why it matters** (1-2 sentences in plain English)
- **A specific action** ("Send a review request link to your last 10 customers — use the QR code generator in your GBP dashboard")
- **Difficulty** — Easy (5–10 min), Medium (30–60 min), or Hard (multi-week effort)
- **Impact** — exact points it would unlock if completed
- **Optional deep link** to where the action is taken (business.google.com, etc.)

The list is sorted by impact (highest first) so the owner sees the biggest wins at the top. The system currently knows about **16 distinct recommendations** covering all 5 pillars.

The dashboard renders this as an expandable checklist below the audit card.

## The Content Generator

Most of the recommendations require the business to add content to their website. We don't make them write it. After they run their first audit, they can click "Generate Content" and instantly get:

1. **A 150–200 word business description** — optimized to be quoted by AI engines, name-drops their city and services.
2. **5 FAQ Q&A pairs** — written in the question-and-answer format AI engines prefer.
3. **A LocalBusiness JSON-LD schema markup** — ready to paste into their website's `<head>` tag.
4. **A 150-character social media bio** — for Instagram, Facebook, LinkedIn.

Each block has a one-click copy button. The content is regenerated against their latest audit so it adapts as their score improves.

## Score History & Monthly Auto-Audits

The single audit is interesting. The trend over time is what builds trust.

- **Score-over-time chart** — A small line chart in the dashboard sidebar shows the last 6 audits. Owners can see whether they're improving and whether the actions they took moved the needle.
- **Monthly auto-audits** — On the 1st of each month at 9 AM, the system re-runs an audit for every business automatically. The owner doesn't need to remember to check.
- **Score-change alerts** — When a score moves by **±10 points or more** between audits (manual or auto), an email is sent to the owner with the old score, the new score, and a link to see what changed.

This turns LeapOne from "a tool I check once" into "a service that's working in the background for me."

## What an SMB owner sees, end-to-end

1. **Sign up** → quick onboarding asks for business name, type, city, province, website.
2. **First audit** → ~30–60 seconds. They see the score, the pillar breakdown, and the recommendations list.
3. **They click "Generate Content"** → instant description, FAQs, schema, social bio.
4. **They follow the top 1–3 recommendations** over the week — claim GBP, ask for reviews, paste the schema on their site.
5. **They re-run the audit** → score moves up, the chart shows progress, completed recommendations drop off.
6. **The first of the next month** → automatic re-audit runs. If their score moved ±10 points, they get an email.

## Why this is different from "just SEO"

Traditional SEO tools optimize for ranked-list search results (the blue links). LeapOne AEO optimizes for **answer-engine search** — the new way people search:

- "Hey ChatGPT, where should I get my car serviced near Milton?"
- "Perplexity, what's the best physiotherapy clinic in Oakville?"
- "OK Google, what time does that bakery on Main Street open?"

These engines don't return 10 blue links. They return **one answer**. Either you're in it or you're not. Optimizing for AI search is a different game with different signals (schema, GBP completeness, review recency, citation graph), and that is what LeapOne measures.

## What's not in Phase 1 (deliberately)

We are NOT trying to be:
- **A review-management tool** — Phase 3, when Google API access is approved (~July 2026)
- **A full SEO suite** — adjacent, but a different category
- **An AI assistant for everything** — focused on AEO/local visibility only

The strategic discipline is to do **one thing extremely well first**: tell SMBs whether AI search engines can see them, and how to fix it. Everything else is either ahead or behind that.

## What sells the product (the trust story)

For a small business owner, the buying decision is mostly about trust. Three things to keep emphasizing:

1. **"We measure what you can actually control."** No black-box vanity metrics. Every signal in the score is something the owner can verify themselves (look at your GBP, look at your website schema, look at your reviews).

2. **"We tell you what to do, not just what's broken."** Every gap produces a specific, achievable action with an effort estimate.

3. **"We're working in the background."** Monthly auto-audits + score-change alerts mean the owner doesn't have to remember us — we remind them when something material changes.

These three are the marketing skeleton.
