# ChatGPT Visibility — Business Overview

## What We Added

LeapOne now audits your business across **three AI engines**: ChatGPT, Perplexity, and Google AI Overview.

The landing page always promised all three. This feature delivers on that promise.

---

## Why ChatGPT Matters

ChatGPT is the world's most-used AI assistant with over 100 million weekly users. When a customer asks "What's the best physiotherapy clinic in Ottawa?" or "Where should I get my hair done in Montreal?", ChatGPT answers from its training knowledge — and if your business isn't in that knowledge, you're invisible to those customers.

Unlike Google Search (where you can rank overnight with the right changes), ChatGPT learns from a fixed snapshot of the internet. That makes appearing in ChatGPT a **long-term brand authority signal** — the businesses that show up are the ones that have built a real web presence over time.

---

## How It Works in the Product

### The Audit
Every time a customer runs an AEO audit, LeapOne now queries ChatGPT with three local search questions:
- "Best [business type] in [city], [province]"
- "[Business type] near [city]"
- "Top [business type] [city] [province]"

LeapOne checks whether the business name appears in ChatGPT's answers and records:
- **Mentioned / Not mentioned**
- The exact snippet where the business appeared (if mentioned)

### The Score
ChatGPT is one of three signals in the **AI Citations pillar** (max 18 points):

| Signal | Points |
|---|---|
| Mentioned by ChatGPT | 6 pts |
| Mentioned by Perplexity | 6 pts |
| Mentioned by Google AI Overview | 6 pts |

Previously, Perplexity was worth 10 pts and Google AI was worth 8 pts. The redistribution keeps the total score at /100 while giving equal weight to all three AI engines — matching how we describe the product on the landing page.

### The Recommendations
If ChatGPT doesn't mention the business, LeapOne generates a specific action plan explaining what to do — and importantly, **why the timeline is different** from Perplexity or Google:

> *"ChatGPT answers from training data, not live search. These actions build the web footprint that gets picked up in future AI model updates (typically 6–12 months)."*

Recommended actions:
1. Claim and fully complete Yelp, TripAdvisor, and Yellow Pages profiles
2. Get listed in the local Chamber of Commerce and BBB
3. Get mentioned in a local news article or industry publication
4. Add a detailed FAQ page to the website

### The "Why This Score?" Drawer
In the audit card, the AI Citations breakdown now shows all three sources with their result. If ChatGPT didn't mention the business, a note explains the training data timeline so the owner isn't confused about why their score hasn't changed yet.

## Honest Framing (Important for Customer Trust)

ChatGPT is fundamentally different from Perplexity and Google AI Overview:

| | ChatGPT | Perplexity | Google AI Overview |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Source** | Training data (fixed) | Live web search | Live web search + Google index |
| **Update speed** | 6–18 months (model releases) | Real-time | Real-time |
| **What influences it** | Authority + citations over time | Current web content | GBP + schema + content |

We are transparent about this in the UI. Customers need to understand that a ChatGPT recommendation is a **long-term investment**, not a quick fix. Being honest about this builds trust and sets realistic expectations.

---

## Impact on Existing Customers

Existing audit scores will change slightly when customers re-run their audit. The scoring redistribution (from Perplexity 10 + Google AI 8 → equal 6+6+6) means:
- Businesses that were only mentioned by Perplexity may see their ai_citation score stay the same or decrease slightly
- Businesses mentioned by ChatGPT gain points they didn't have before
- Total possible score remains 100

This is a one-time adjustment. After the first re-run, the new formula is the baseline.
