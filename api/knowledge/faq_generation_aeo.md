# FAQ generation — 2026 AEO best practices

These guidelines determine which FAQs actually get cited by ChatGPT,
Perplexity, and Google AI Overview. Apply them to every Q&A you produce.

## 1. Question phrasing — long-tail and conversational

Real-world AI search queries are conversational and specific. Generic
questions match generic queries; specific questions match specific high-
intent queries.

**Bad (generic):**
- "What are your hours?"
- "How much does it cost?"
- "Do you offer X?"

**Good (long-tail, specific):**
- "Are you open on weekends in <city>?"
- "How much does emergency <service> cost on weekends in the <region>?"
- "Do you accept <specific insurance plan> for <specific procedure>?"

Aim for question lengths of 8-15 words. Always include the city or
neighborhood when geographic context is relevant.

## 2. Geographic and temporal modifiers

Include city, neighborhood, "near me", province, or year when relevant.
These modifiers match how customers actually phrase queries to AI engines.

- "in <city>"
- "in 2026"
- "near <neighborhood>"
- "in <province>" (use abbreviations and full names — "ON" + "Ontario")

For Canadian businesses, postal-code prefixes (FSAs) like "K1P" are also
real customer search patterns.

## 3. Answer structure — direct first, context second

The first sentence of every answer must be the **direct answer** to the
question, citation-able as a standalone sentence. The rest of the answer
provides context.

**Bad:**
> "We have been serving the Ottawa area since 2015 and our family-owned
> business takes pride in offering competitive pricing. Our standard
> consultations start at $120."

**Good:**
> "Our standard consultations start at $120 in Ottawa. We've offered the
> same transparent pricing since 2015 with no hidden fees, and emergency
> after-hours visits are billed at $180."

AI engines extract the first sentence as the citable answer. If it
doesn't contain the answer, you don't get cited.

## 4. Include named entities

Mention specific names and these names get extracted by AI engines:
- The business name itself
- Specific service names (not "treatments" — "Invisalign treatment", not "repairs" — "tankless water heater installation")
- Neighborhood names where the business operates
- Certifications (e.g., "Red Seal certified", "RECO licensed", "CRPO registered")
- Partner brands or platforms ("we accept Sunlife and Manulife", "verified on HomeStars")

Generic answers don't get cited. Named-entity-rich answers do.

## 5. Concrete numbers when available

Include at least one specific number per answer when relevant — prices,
durations, distances, sizes, counts. Numbers get extracted explicitly:
- "Average cost is $120-180"
- "Typically takes 30-45 minutes"
- "Within a 25 km radius of <city>"
- "We've completed over 1,200 projects since 2018"

Numbers tell AI engines this is a factual answer worth citing rather
than vague marketing copy.

## 6. Question variations — same intent, different phrasing

For the most-searched topics (pricing, availability, what's included),
generate **2-3 question variations** that ask the same thing different
ways. AI engines match different query phrasings — covering more
phrasings catches more queries.

For pricing alone:
- "How much does <service> cost in <city>?"
- "What's the price of <service> in <city>?"
- "<service> price in <city>"

Same answer can serve all three (or vary slightly to feel natural).

## 7. Cover comparison and decision questions

These are HIGH-INTENT queries that convert well. Always include at least
one of each per FAQ set:

**Comparison:**
- "<service A> vs <service B> — which one do I need?"
- "Best <service> for <use case>?"

**Decision:**
- "How do I know if I need <service>?"
- "When should I call a <profession>?"
- "Should I get <X> or wait?"

## 8. Include "what we don't do" answers

Counter-intuitive but powerful. Include 1-2 questions about services or
situations the business does NOT cover, and answer honestly. Example:

- Q: "Do you do commercial roofing?"
- A: "No, we focus on residential roofing only. For commercial projects,
  we recommend reaching out to a contractor specializing in commercial
  installations — your project size and code requirements differ from
  residential work."

This builds trust, answers "is this for me?" queries, and AI engines
pick up the negative info as factual context.

## 9. Use second-person voice

Write the answer to "you" (the customer). Direct engagement, conversational
tone:
- ✓ "You'll need to bring your prior X-rays to your first appointment."
- ✗ "Patients are required to bring their prior X-rays."

## 10. Answer length: 40-80 words is the sweet spot

- Under 40 words: too short, AI engines may pass over for a more
  substantive answer
- 40-80 words: ideal — substantive enough to be authoritative, short
  enough to be cited verbatim
- Over 100 words: too long, AI engines may truncate or skip for
  a tighter alternative

Stay tight. Resist the urge to add marketing fluff.

## 11. Avoid keyword stuffing

Natural language only. Never repeat the same keyword more than 2-3 times
in a single answer. If you find yourself starting consecutive sentences
with the same noun, rewrite.

AI engines actively penalize keyword-stuffed content — they're trained to
recognize it as low-quality.

## 12. Time-sensitive language signals freshness

Including "as of 2026", "current pricing", "this year" is a positive
freshness signal for AI engines. They prefer to cite content that
explicitly claims to be current. Use sparingly — once or twice across
the full FAQ set is enough.

---

## Quick checklist for every Q&A you generate

Before approving an FAQ, scan it against these:
- [ ] Question is 8-15 words and includes geo/temporal modifier when relevant
- [ ] First sentence of answer is the direct factual answer
- [ ] Answer mentions at least one named entity (business, service, place, certification)
- [ ] Answer includes at least one concrete number when relevant
- [ ] Answer is 40-80 words
- [ ] Tone is second-person, conversational
- [ ] No keyword stuffing
- [ ] At least one comparison or decision question is in the full set
- [ ] At least one "we don't do X" answer is in the full set
