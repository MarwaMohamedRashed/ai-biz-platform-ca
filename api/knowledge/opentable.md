# OpenTable — what to know when guiding the owner

## What it is, briefly
OpenTable is the dominant restaurant-discovery + reservations platform
in Canada and North America. Free directory listing without enabling
reservations. AI engines (especially Perplexity and Google AI Overview)
cite OpenTable heavily for "best restaurant in <city>" queries — even
for restaurants that don't take OpenTable bookings.

## Two tiers — the owner needs to understand the choice

**Tier 1 — Free directory listing**
- Restaurant appears on OpenTable.ca + OpenTable.com
- AI engines cite it
- Customers see hours, photos, menu, neighborhood
- "Reserve" button is hidden (no booking flow)
- $0/month, no commission

**Tier 2 — Paid reservations (OpenTable Connect or Pro)**
- All of the above
- "Reserve" button enabled — customers book through OpenTable
- Commission per cover (varies by tier; ~$1-2 per seated diner)
- Synced calendar, no double-bookings

For AEO purposes alone, **Tier 1 is enough.** The owner doesn't need to
enable reservations to get the AI citation benefit.

## Signup specifics

1. Go to restaurant.opentable.com
2. Click "Get Started" → "Find Diners" (this is the free listing path)
3. Fill in: restaurant name, address, phone, hours, cuisine, price
   range, photos
4. Submit for review — OpenTable confirms the address (must be a
   physical restaurant, not catering-only or food truck)
5. Approval takes 3-5 business days

## Common stuck points (volunteer if owner gets stuck)

| Stuck point | What to say |
|---|---|
| "OpenTable is pushing me to enable reservations" | The free directory tier is real but they highlight the paid tier. Look for "Find Diners" or "Discovery only" option in the signup flow. If it's hidden, email support@opentable.com asking specifically for "directory listing only, no reservations." |
| "We're a food truck / pop-up / catering-only" | OpenTable requires a fixed physical location. If you don't have one, this isn't the right directory — focus on Yelp, TripAdvisor, and Instagram instead. |
| "Cuisine isn't in their list" | Pick the closest match + add specifics in the description (e.g., "Indo-Chinese fusion" → cuisine "Asian Fusion" + describe in the bio). |
| "I want to show my menu" | OpenTable supports menu uploads (PDF or text). Owner adds via dashboard → Menu section. AI engines actually read these. |

## Pairing with TripAdvisor
For restaurants, the OpenTable + TripAdvisor pair is the high-leverage
combo. OpenTable is cited for "where should I eat" queries; TripAdvisor
is cited for "is this place good?" queries. Different intents, both
valuable.

## Why the free tier is genuinely enough
OpenTable's commission-per-cover pricing makes the paid tier expensive
for thin-margin restaurants. AEO citation benefit is identical between
free and paid — the rich-result snippet, the AI engine's ability to
quote your description, all that comes from the directory listing
itself, not the booking integration. Don't push the owner to upgrade
unless they're separately interested in OpenTable's customer
acquisition channel.
