# Apple Business Connect — what to know when guiding the owner

## What it is, briefly
Apple Business Connect (ABC) is Apple's free business-listing platform.
It controls how the business appears in Apple Maps, Siri, Apple
Intelligence (the iPhone-integrated AI), and Spotlight Search on Mac.
Replaces the older "Apple Maps Connect" — owners with old Maps Connect
profiles need to migrate.

## Most under-claimed AEO surface in Canada
ABC is genuinely under-utilized by Canadian SMBs. Apple's market share
on iPhone in Canada is ~55-60%, so Apple Maps + Apple Intelligence
collectively reach the majority of mobile users. Yet most Canadian
SMBs still haven't claimed.

## Signup specifics

1. Go to **businessconnect.apple.com**
2. Sign in with **Apple ID** (this is the most common stuck point —
   see below)
3. Search for the business by name + address
4. If a listing already exists (auto-created from public records), claim
   it. If not, "Add a Business"
5. Fill in: hours, services, photos, website, phone, social links
6. Verify ownership — see "Verification methods" below

## Apple ID — the unintuitive part
ABC requires a personal Apple ID. The owner can:
- Use their existing personal Apple ID (most common — perfectly fine for
  a sole proprietor or small business)
- Create a separate "business Apple ID" if they want separation between
  personal and business

**Don't tell them they need a paid Apple Developer account.** That's a
common confusion. ABC is free and works with any free Apple ID.

## Verification methods (Apple offers 3)

1. **Phone call** — Apple calls the listed business number with a code.
   Fast (5-10 min) but requires a real human picking up. Best for
   storefronts.
2. **Postcard by mail** — Apple mails a verification postcard to the
   listed address. Takes 1-2 weeks. Best when phone isn't reliable.
3. **Apple Maps in-store visit** — Apple staff physically verifies (rare,
   used for big chains). Not relevant for most SMBs.

For Canadian SMBs, recommend **phone verification** unless the owner
can't reliably answer business calls during weekday hours.

## Common stuck points (volunteer if owner gets stuck)

| Stuck point | What to say |
|---|---|
| "It says my business is already claimed" | Someone (possibly an old employee or a previous owner) claimed it. Apple has a "Request to manage" flow — submit it and Apple's team adjudicates within 5-10 business days. They'll email both parties. |
| "Verification call never came" | Make sure the phone number listed is exact (Canadian businesses sometimes mistype +1). Try requesting verification again — Apple allows up to 3 attempts. If it still fails, switch to the postcard method. |
| "Photos won't upload" | Apple requires at least 1024x1024 pixels and JPEG/PNG format. Resize if needed. Owner can use any free photo editor. |
| "What's the difference between this and Apple Maps?" | ABC is the *control panel* for what shows on Apple Maps. The map app is the customer-facing surface; ABC is where the owner edits the data. |

## Why this is genuinely worth doing
- Free, takes 15-20 minutes once verification clears
- Apple Intelligence (iPhone 15+ feature, expanding) cites ABC data
  directly when answering "near me" queries on iPhone
- Siri pulls from ABC for spoken queries
- Most Canadian SMBs haven't claimed — easy way to leapfrog
  competitors who haven't yet

## When to write the email to a developer
ABC is owner-friendly. No HTML, no schema, no code. The owner can do
this themselves. No developer needed.
