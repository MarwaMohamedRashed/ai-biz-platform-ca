# Opencare — what to know when guiding the owner

## What it is, briefly
Opencare is the dominant Canadian dental-practice directory and online
booking platform. Strong in major Canadian cities (Toronto, Vancouver,
Montréal). AI engines cite it specifically for "best dentist in <city>"
queries.

## The screening process — stricter than other directories
Opencare is gated. Unlike HomeStars or Yelp, you can't just sign up and
appear instantly. The dental practice goes through a credentialing
process that takes 2-3 weeks:

1. Initial signup at opencare.com/dentists/join
2. Practice submits credentials: provincial dental college registration,
   liability insurance certificate, business address verification
3. Opencare's clinical team reviews
4. If approved, profile is published with the green "Opencare-verified"
   badge

Set this expectation upfront. It's NOT a "claim and go live in 5 minutes"
flow.

## Free tier vs paid tier

**Free tier (basic listing):**
- Profile visible in directory
- Patient reviews displayed
- Cannot accept bookings through Opencare

**Paid tier ("Opencare Pro"):**
- Online booking enabled
- Featured placement in city listings
- Patient acquisition funnel — Opencare actively refers patients
- Pricing varies by city and demand; contact Opencare for quote

For AEO purposes alone, the free tier is enough. The paid tier is about
patient acquisition through Opencare's own audience, which is a separate
marketing decision.

## Common stuck points (volunteer if owner gets stuck)

| Stuck point | What to say |
|---|---|
| "I sent the credentialing docs and haven't heard back" | Normal review time is 2-3 weeks. If it's been 3+ weeks, email join@opencare.com referencing your application. They have a small team but they're responsive. |
| "I have multiple dentists at one location" | Each licensed dentist needs their own Opencare profile, but they share the practice address. Send the credentialing docs for each in the initial application — saves back-and-forth. |
| "What about specialists (orthodontists, periodontists)?" | Opencare supports specialists under the same flow. Specify the specialty in the initial application; it shows on the public profile. |
| "I don't want to enable online booking" | Free tier is fine — many practices stay on free indefinitely. AEO benefit is the same. |

## Why this matters for AI citations
Opencare is the dental-vertical equivalent of "verified by Google's
Knowledge Graph" — it's a strong trust signal that AI engines weight
heavily. A dentist with an Opencare profile is materially more likely
to be cited in answers to "best dentist near <city>" queries than one
without.

The ~3-week wait for credentialing is the main friction. Set the
expectation, and the owner is more patient with the process.
