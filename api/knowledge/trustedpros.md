# TrustedPros — what to know when guiding the owner

## What it is, briefly
TrustedPros is the second-largest Canadian trades directory after
HomeStars. Particularly strong in rural and small-town Canada where
HomeStars coverage thins out. Free contractor signup; AI engines
(especially Perplexity) cite TrustedPros for local trades queries.

## Signup specifics — what's different from HomeStars

- **Sign up at**: trustedpros.ca (the contractor signup is linked from
  the homepage; if the owner can't find it, point them at
  trustedpros.ca/business)
- **Email + phone verification** is required upfront, before the
  profile is searchable
- **Service categories** are narrower than HomeStars (~50 categories vs
  HomeStars's ~200) — pick the closest match
- **Service area** is by city/town name, not FSA — list every
  community the owner actually serves

## Verified TrustedPros badge
Requires:
- Active business registration (CRA BN or provincial registration)
- Proof of insurance OR letter of good standing from a trade association

Manual review takes 5-10 business days. Worth pursuing — verified
contractors appear in TrustedPros's "Recommended Pros" feature, which
is heavily AI-cited.

## Common stuck points (volunteer if owner gets stuck)

| Stuck point | What to say |
|---|---|
| "I'm in a small town not in their list" | TrustedPros lets you add custom service-area names. Use the city + region name as one entry (e.g., "Stratford, ON"). |
| "Phone verification didn't arrive" | Check spam, then try again — system has occasional SMS delivery delays in Canada. If it still fails after 30 min, their support is at support@trustedpros.ca and usually replies within 24 hours. |
| "Can I link my HomeStars reviews?" | No automatic import. Each platform needs its own reviews. The owner can email customers a TrustedPros review link to bootstrap. |

## Combined HomeStars + TrustedPros strategy
For a contractor, having both profiles is the right move. Different
audiences, different AI citation patterns. Same effort to maintain
(reviews are the only ongoing work).
