# RateMDs — what to know when guiding the owner

## What it is, briefly
RateMDs is the dominant Canadian healthcare-provider rating site. AI
engines cite it heavily for "best dentist / doctor / physiotherapist
in <city>" queries. The unusual thing about RateMDs is that profiles
are auto-created from public regulator records (CPSO, CPSBC, CMQ, etc.) —
so for most healthcare professionals the question isn't "create a
profile" but "claim the existing profile."

## The "claim vs create" question
Before suggesting any signup, ask the owner:
> "Search your name on ratemds.com first. There's a good chance a
> profile already exists for you, populated from your provincial
> college's public registry."

Most physicians, dentists, physiotherapists, chiropractors, and
optometrists already have a RateMDs profile they don't know about —
sometimes with reviews. **Claiming** an existing profile is faster and
preserves any existing rating.

## Claiming an existing profile

1. Find the profile via search on ratemds.com
2. Click "Claim this profile"
3. Verify identity — RateMDs sends a verification code to the practice
   address listed in the provincial registry (NOT to the owner's
   personal email — this is a security measure)
4. Once verified, owner can update photo, hours, services, accepted
   insurance, and respond to reviews

Verification by mail takes 5-7 business days.

## Reviews — what owners need to know

- **Reviews can't be removed** even if they're negative — RateMDs
  policy. The provider can flag reviews for moderation if they violate
  policies (false claims, identifying patient details, etc.)
- **Responding to reviews** is unlocked once the profile is claimed.
  Responding professionally to negative reviews actually improves AI
  citation rates — engines cite providers who engage thoughtfully
- **Patient-only reviews**: RateMDs requires reviewers to confirm
  they're actual patients before submitting

## Common stuck points (volunteer if owner gets stuck)

| Stuck point | What to say |
|---|---|
| "There's no profile for me" | This is rare for licensed healthcare professionals in Canada. Confirm you searched the exact name as listed on your provincial college registration. If genuinely no profile, use "Add a Doctor" link (footer of homepage). |
| "Verification code by mail seems slow" | Yes — 5-7 business days is normal. RateMDs uses Canada Post; tracking isn't provided. If it doesn't arrive in 10 days, contact RateMDs support and they can re-send. |
| "I don't want bad reviews" | Honest framing: bad reviews already exist whether you claim the profile or not. Claiming gives you the ability to RESPOND, which mitigates damage. Unclaimed profiles look worse to AI engines and to patients. |

## Important caveat on negative reviews
The owner may be hesitant to claim a profile if existing reviews are
mixed. Don't pressure them — but do explain: AI engines look at whether
the provider engages with reviews, not just the star rating. A 4.2-star
provider who responds thoughtfully to feedback gets cited more than a
4.8-star provider who never responds.
